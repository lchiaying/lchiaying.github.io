The first year of college - demands of coursework and varied nature of resources - can be an overwhelming experience for many students. If you find yourself having difficulties with nagivating the resources, or having any academic difficulties, I am here to guide you through finding the right resource that will help you attune to the demands of the course.

Course material related

  • Course textbook: A wealth of information. Read it!
  • Suggested problems: Do them even if they do not need to be turned in.
  • Past exams This is what an exam paper will look like. For practice.
  • Past exam questions/answers and Answers in PDF format

Need a question answered?

  • Office hours: Drop in any time during office hours, for as long as you need.
  • Workshop: Your opportunity to work through problems and ask questions.
  • Piazza Online forums for questions, discussion and answers (sign up req'd with access code: MATH184)
  • Math Learning Center The coolest place to hang out. Get constant and instant help for your math questions.
  • AMS Tutoring Service: Free tutoring, appointment tutoring (not free), tutor registry
  • Your classmate: There's no better way to learn than to teach and learn from a friend. (However, be mindful of academic misconduct; i.e. copying/cheating)
  • Private tutoring: If you feel the need to enlist a private tutor, the Math office may have pointers to getting a tutor. Also check out AMS Tutoring.

Online resources

  • Funny Little Calculus Text: For a funny little bit of bedside reading.
  • A Review of Algebra concepts Come back to this throughout the term to strengthen your algebra skills.
  • Wolfram Alpha Useful! Go check it out
